Introduction

Nabla - is an object denoted with the $\nabla$ symbol. In cartesian coordinates it is defined as
$\displaystyle \nabla = \mathbf{i} \frac{\partial}{\partial x} + \mathbf{j} \frac{\partial}{\partial y} + \mathbf{k} \frac{\partial}{\partial z}$
(The definition varies depending on the coordinate system).
It allows us to calculate the slope of a field, and specify three useful operators which can act on vector or scalar fields: gradient, divergence and curl.

The gradient of a scalar field, $F$ is represented as $\nabla F$ and gives a vector as the result.
The divergence of a vector field $\mathbf{F}$ is represented as $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}$ and, when applied to a point, gives a scalar as the result.
The curl of a vector field $\mathbf{F}$ is represented as $\nabla \times \mathbf{F}$ and, when applied to a point, returns a vector.

If the divergence and curl of a vector field are known, then these contain all the necessary information to reconstruct the original field.

The divergence theorem can be used to convert between integral (macroscopic) and differential (microscopic) forms of equations involving divergence, whereas Stokes' Theorem is used to convert between integral and differential forms of equations involving curl.
